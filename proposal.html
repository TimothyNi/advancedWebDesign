Describe the targeted user(s) using a persona:

http://www.smartinsights.com/marketplace-analysis/customer-analysis/web-design-personas/

[ask the client for this information, don't guess.]

 

That website above states the following about Personas: A simple definition of a web persona is: "a summary of the characteristics, needs, motivations and environment of a key type of website user".  This is a great way to look at your user base for a website and will help all of us understand how we should be building this website.

 

The user we are focused on is not the client we are working with, but three of her clients.

 

1st we are most focused on the Persona of what I would call “Average Customer”.  A person who is possibly thinking of going to Ginkgo Coffee, or a person who goes there regularly already.  A person who has an interest in what is available to eat / drink at the coffee shop, or what bands will be playing there soon.

 

2nd we are focused on the customer that has an interest in the catering, and I’d call them “Catering Customer”.  They don’t have an interest in much of the site but are more focused on the catering aspect.  They could be returning customers, or new customers doing research, and we want the catering to be as easily readable as possible for this reason.

 

The 3rd customer we are focused on is not technically a customer of the store, but a band looking to book a gig at the store.  They will be known as “Band Customers”.  We want to make it as easy to book a gig with Kathy as it is to set up a catering deal.

 

There are obviously many different personas that could visit the site, but we will focus on these three.

 

 

List the main menu items the client would like and describe the general contents of each.

 

The navigation menu will consist of several links to different parts of the site.   Web layout, color schemes, and wireframe are still in developmental stages.   The menu options most likely link to a menu page, an events page, a contact us/about us page (we haven’t determined yet if it is necessary to have two separate pages or if we can combine the information in both pages), and a section on organic coffee.

 

The client, Kathy, would like a simple and inviting page that highlights a sense of the community the shop represents without any splashy and/or poppy themes.  She would also like to have a way to list events on her page on a calendar, forms that allow event registration, catering orders, and a contact us form for general questions.

 

 

Describe how eCommerce will be incorporated into the site (product or service, registration for events, etc.)

 

Our client would like her customers to be able to pay for catering on her site and buy tickets to the concerts. We will use PayPal as a placeholder for how we accept money online. There will be a bank account that the money will go to and PayPal will send them the receipt. For the catering, the form will add up each item they select and charge them for the delivery. If PayPal adds its own fee, there will be a noticed that it is being added. For tickets, it will take the price and times it by how many are being bought.

 

 

Describe how a database will be used as part of the site design.

For example, user list, customer list, inventory of products, FAQ, or user content are all good candidates for database storage.

 

A database that we will use as part of the site design will be for the catering.  This database will include items from the menu that Kathy can keep track of when she is filling a request for a catering service.  The site will allow her to pull items from the database based on the customer’s needs. 

 

There will also be a database of the performers that will be performing at the site.  There will be a page that allows for Kathy to enter the names of people that will be performing at the shop, and the time/date they will be performing.  This will allow for organization of music performances at the shop.

 

Another possible database that will be incorporated into the site design is a database for the hours that they are open because these hours might change due to a variety of reasons, such as shortage of staff, different among the different locations, or different weekend hours.
